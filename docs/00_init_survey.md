1. Who is the primary user?
  - kids using it directly

  2. What age range are you targeting?
  - 10-14

  3. What should the first version actually do?
  - choose breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack
  - enter ingredients they already have
  - filter by allergies or dietary needs
  - get one recipe at a time with guided steps
  - save favorites

  4. How should recipe guidance work?
  - simple text steps
  - one step at a time with big buttons
  - timers built into steps

  5. How “kid-safe” should the recipes be?
  - avoid stove/oven recipes for younger kids
  - mark steps that require adult help
  - block knife-heavy recipes
  - offer age-based safety modes

  6. Where will recipes come from?
  - recipes generated dynamically by AI running locally
  - a mix of curated recipes plus AI suggestions

  7. How personalized should suggestions be?
  - favorite foods
  - disliked ingredients
  - allergies
  - cooking skill level
  - available appliances like microwave, toaster, air fryer

  8. Do you want accounts in v1?
  - no accounts. this is for local use only

  9. What should the visual style feel like?
  - more calm and educational
  - game-like with badges/rewards

  10. Do you want gamification?
  - badges for completed recipes
  - streaks
  - level progression
  - stars for trying new foods

  11. What is the iPhone delivery plan?
  - a web app first running on local networked machine that works well on iPhone Safari

  12. Do you want the web version to work offline?
  - partially offline with local llms

  13. Will the app need backend services in v1?
  - recipe storage
  - AI recommendations
  - analytics
  - parent dashboard

  14. Should AI chat be part of the experience?
  - yes with great safety guards. this must work with local ai. if remote ai chat is required then a parent must supervise

  15. What does “ported onto other machines” mean for you?
  -  local networked machine that is accessible on local networked mobile devices

  16. What are your must-have constraints?
  - no paid APIs except approved llm access
  - child privacy requirements
  - no ads
  - no user-generated content
  - parental guidance for remote llm
